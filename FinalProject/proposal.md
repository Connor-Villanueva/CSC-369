# Final Project Proposal

### The Epstein Files


1. **What questions are you trying to answer?**

    I want to answer the following questions and more as they come up:

- What connections can I make between people who appear in the files?

- For those connections, how long did they last? How often were they communicating with one another?

- What kind of language is used? Can this language be used to identify suspicious behavior?

2. **Why is this question worth answering? This can be either because it has some personal value to you or because it is a question that others are asking. If it is a question others are asking, then provide an example.**

    I think these questions are worth answering because this is a major, and very sensitive, topic amongst our generation currently. With the sensitivity of the topic, I feel that people stray away from analyzing the data out of fear of the uncomfortable/unsettling feeling that comes from it.

3. **What is your hypothesis? What leads you towards that hypothesis?**

    Due to the nature of these questions, there's not much of a hypothesis to make. I think that the amount of connections that I may be able to make will be surprising despite the amount of censorship. 
    
    I'm especially interested in performing an analysis on the language used in the data and believe that I will find evidence suggesting disturbing actions.
